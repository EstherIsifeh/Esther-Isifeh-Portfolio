Context

Nigeria's most ambitious
digital skills programme.

The Three Million Technical Talent (3MTT) programme is a flagship initiative by the Federal Ministry of Communication, Innovation, and Digital Economy — designed to build a pool of three million technically skilled Nigerians to power the country's digital economy by 2027.

WorkNigeria was selected as a delivery partner, and I led the product work to build the dedicated job board and learner-matching platform that sits at the heart of the programme's digital infrastructure.

The scale of the challenge

Building a product for a government programme of this magnitude meant managing complex stakeholder requirements, tight timelines, and the expectation of reliability at national scale — while keeping the experience simple and accessible for first-time tech learners across Nigeria.

What We Built

Platform that matches
fellows to opportunity.

The 3MTT platform on WorkNigeria enables fellows (programme participants) to register, explore learning pathways, connect with training providers, and access job opportunities — all in one place.

Fellow registration

Onboarding at scale

Designed a clear, accessible onboarding flow for learners across different states and digital literacy levels — ensuring anyone could register and get started.

Provider matching

Connecting fellows to employers

Built the employer dashboard that connects fellows with verified employers based on track, location, and availability.

Job board

Dedicated 3MTT job listings

Created a curated job board for 3MTT fellows — featuring roles specifically relevant to their newly acquired technical skills and career stage.

Progress tracking

Programme visibility

Enabled visibility into fellow progress — supporting the Ministry's goal of tracking programme uptake and outcomes at a national level.

Challenges & How I Navigated Them

What made this
hard — and what I did.

  • 🏛️
    Government stakeholder complexity. Working with a Federal Ministry meant multiple layers of sign-off and requirements that evolved over time. I managed this through clear documentation, regular alignment calls, and maintaining a transparent roadmap that all stakeholders could track.
  • ⚡
    Tight delivery timelines. The programme had public-facing milestones and launch dates tied to government communications. I worked closely with engineering to prioritise ruthlessly and phase delivery — shipping a functional MVP first, then iterating.
  • 🌍
    Diverse, distributed user base. Fellows came from 36 states, with varying levels of digital literacy. I pushed for a simple, forgiving UX — minimal jargon, clear calls to action, and accessible design — to ensure the platform worked for everyone, not just tech-comfortable users.
  • 🔗
    Integration with existing WorkNigeria infrastructure. The 3MTT platform needed to live within WorkNigeria's existing product without disrupting existing users. I mapped the integration points carefully and defined clear boundaries between the 3MTT experience and the core platform.
